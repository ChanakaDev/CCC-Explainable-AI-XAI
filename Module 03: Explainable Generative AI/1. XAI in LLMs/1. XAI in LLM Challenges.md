# XAI in LLM Challenges

## Framing

These notes follow the categorization of LLM explainability proposed by **Zhao et al. (2023)**. Coverage in this module:

- XAI in **fine-tuning**
- XAI in **prompting**
- XAI in **knowledge augmentation** (Retrieval-Augmented Generation, RAG)

## Why XAI is hard for LLMs

Four core challenges make explainability difficult for large language models:

1. **Scale and complexity.** Models like GPT-4 have an unknown parameter count, likely in the trillions. Tracing reasoning end-to-end is intractable.
2. **Entangled, abstract representations.** LLMs learn high-dimensional, distributed representations. Concepts are not cleanly separated into individual neurons or layers.
3. **No ground truth.** There is rarely (or never) a "correct" explanation to compare against. This makes it hard to:
   - Design explanation algorithms that truly reflect the model's decision process.
   - Evaluate **faithfulness** (does the explanation match the model's actual reasoning?) and **fidelity**.
4. **Lack of transparency.** Many LLMs are closed: architecture, weights, and training data are undocumented or proprietary, which limits what explanation methods can be applied.

## Caveat: a moving target

XAI for LLMs is a **rapidly evolving field**. The methods described in this module reflect a snapshot of current research and may be outdated within months. Treat this material as a starting point — read recent papers to stay current.
